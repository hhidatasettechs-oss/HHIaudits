BOUNDARY TEST — INTERMITTENT INITIATION WITHDRAWAL PATTERN  

Trigger: Repeated contact initiated after prolonged absence, coinciding with physical access intent.

Behavioral Indicators:
- Communication only initiated when access was convenient or desired.
- Justification of disengagement through non-verifiable circumstantial claims (e.g. illness, scheduling).
- No continuity of interaction outside access windows.
- Recurring cycle of engagement → access → rapid disengagement.

Governance Assessment:
- Pattern classified under: INITIATION-WITHDRAWAL LOOP
- Drift type: Bypass Behavior (see HHI-GOV-01 §3.1.2)
- STOP protocol validated and exercised.
